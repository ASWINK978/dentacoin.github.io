Crypto wallets are a way to access, store, send, receive and track digital currency holdings whether it is Dentacoin, Ethereum, Civic, Bitcoin, etc.
Cryptocurrency wallets are like the leather wallets which you use for carrying cash and credit cards, the only difference is that the crypto wallet can hold hundreds of digital currencies with an additional option of keep tracking details such as when, where and how much you spent, added or withdrew.


How does it work ?

Cryptocurrency wallets is like the safe box you use at home to store your jewelry or very important documents. Losing the key of the safe box, means that you have lost your ownership for your valuables if it end up in the wrong hands.Cryptocurrency wallet work pretty much the same. Instead of the physical key, you are to save your digital key.
This digital key is known as “Private Key“ or “Master Key “ which takes the form of hexadecimal codes, similar to: 3a1076bf45ab87712ad64ccb3b10217737f7faacbf2872e88fdd9a537d8fe266

Important ! The private key is the most important data for accessing your wallet. If you lose it, there is no way it could be restored. EVER. Therefore, you should take the appropriate steps to secure it such writing it, print it on paper, type it as a document or convert it to an image or even memorized !

How to create the wallet ?

All crypto wallets works almost the same.As an example, we have attached a tutorial on how to create wallet on Jaxx. After signing in, every wallet will display a learning tutorial on how to create the wallet. Please don’t miss to check their FAQ page.  
To send or receive cryptocurrency, first you need a cryptocurrency wallet, then you need to put in the public address of the sender and recipient. The process of sending and receiving cryptocurrencies like Dentacoin, Ethereum, Bitcoin, etc, can differ slightly between wallets ( as each coin has its own set of wallet options in which that cryptocurrency can be stored):

  • Log into your wallet.
  • Go to the send / receive screen (by clicking the tab or button that says this or shows the proper icon).
  • Choose whether you want to send or receive cryptocurrency. TIP: In general you must only send and receive like-coins. Meaning, you can only send/receive Bitcoin-to-Bitcoin, Litecoin-to-Litecoin, etc. (you can’t, for example, send Bitcoin to an Ethereum wallet).
  • For sending: Enter the public wallet address of the recipient and choose the amount to send (make sure to account for transaction fees; you’ll need enough coins in your wallet to pay the fee). After you confirm the numbers, triple checking them to avoid silly mistakes then hit “send transaction” (or the equivalent) and verify the transaction one last time (confirming your public address and their public address is correct). TIP: you can write a note with your transaction to let the recipient know what transaction is for.
  • For receiving: You don’t have to do anything except share your public wallet address with the sender. If you are in person, you can do this by letting them scan a QR code (if your wallet offers that).
If you are still uncertain, just look up the FAQ for the wallet you are using to verify you have the steps down. Check out the video below for more.

TIP: You can use a platform like Changelly or Shapeshift to turn one type of cryptocurrency into another. That can be helpful if you have Dentacoin, but want to do a transaction in another coin.

TIP: A wallet’s public address (or “public key”) looks like this:
DCN Wallet: 0xf902fd8B2AEE76AE81bBA106d667cCF368C2f9A1…
Private keys look different.

You should NEVER share your private key, but as you can tell from our share above, sharing your public wallet address is not a problem. So again, do share the public address that looks like that (the worst that can happen is someone puts coins in it), but NEVER share your private key or password (as that is like handing a stranger your wallet). Cryptocurrencies are treated very differently in various countries. While they receive favorable treatment in some parts of the world, others have moved to curtail or even ban their use and trade. Please check your current country status in relation to cryptocurrency.
Cryptocurrenices are similar to e-money which means that they can face similar risks as classic e-payment systems. More concretely:

Phishing information. Users can be tricked into going to a phishing website which looks exactly the same as the original one. After entering their password (private key), all their assets are stolen. Please always verify the URL address of the website you are entering your private key; always verify any tempting offer you see and never trust any individual who is asking for your private key.

Hacking a payment gateway
  • User address error. An error in the address to which the money transfer is made could cause loss of money. Example: the last digit of the address was not copied or was copied with a different letter/number, the money would disappear or be sent to another wallet. Those transactions are irreversible. Before sending, double-check the recipient’s address (at least check the first and last characters), the amount being sent, and the size of the associated fee.
  • Loss/theft of a wallet file/ private key. Most users store their cryptocurrency wallet files/ private keys on their computers. Therefore, they can be stolen using malware or lost if the hard disk crashes. Please always keep a simple printed copy of your most sensitive wallet data as a backup on a secure place. You can also consider hardware wallet options such as TREZOR, Ledger Nano S.
  • Insecure ICOs (Initial coin offerings) . This type of fundraising called an ICO - Initial Coin Offering - has gained great popularity throughout the last year. What does it mean exactly? You send funds (most likely in another cryptocurrency such as Bitcoin and Ether) to a new Blockchain project to support its development and in return - you receive the corresponding amount of their currency (token). Where is the risk? You are never 100% assured to receive the promised, as the market is still not regulated enough. There is no guarantee that the project will succeed and the tokens you’ve bought will increase in value. Keep a cool head and make informed decisions when participating in an ICO, and don’t panic or hurry. Note: This risk has nothing in common with Dentacoin, which has already had its ICO in 2017 and now the team is fully focused on development and global adoption.
